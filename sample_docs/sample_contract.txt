MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT

This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2025 ("Effective Date")
by and between:

  Contoso Technologies, Inc., a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at
  1 Microsoft Way, Redmond, WA 98052 ("Service Provider")

and

  Fabrikam Industries LLC, a Texas limited liability company with its principal place of business at
  500 Main Street, Dallas, TX 75201 ("Client").

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1. SERVICES

1.1 Scope of Services. Service Provider agrees to perform the professional services described in one or
more Statements of Work ("SOW") executed by both parties from time to time. Each SOW shall set forth the
specific deliverables, timelines, and fees applicable to such engagement.

1.2 Change Orders. Any changes to the scope of services must be documented in a written change order
signed by authorized representatives of both parties before work commences.

1.3 Subcontractors. Service Provider may engage subcontractors to perform services under this Agreement,
provided that Service Provider remains responsible for all services performed.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2. PAYMENT TERMS

2.1 Fees. Client shall pay Service Provider the fees set forth in the applicable SOW. Unless otherwise
specified, fees are quoted in U.S. Dollars (USD).

2.2 Invoicing. Service Provider shall invoice Client monthly in arrears. All invoices are due and payable
within thirty (30) days of the invoice date ("Due Date").

2.3 Late Payments. Any amounts not paid by the Due Date shall accrue interest at a rate of 1.5% per month
(18% per annum) or the maximum rate permitted by applicable law, whichever is lower.

2.4 Taxes. Client is responsible for all applicable taxes, levies, or duties associated with the services,
excluding taxes based on Service Provider's income.

2.5 Contract Value. The estimated total contract value for the initial term is $250,000 USD.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3. TERM AND TERMINATION

3.1 Term. This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of
two (2) years, expiring on January 14, 2027 ("Expiration Date"), unless earlier terminated.

3.2 Renewal. This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one-year terms unless either party
provides written notice of non-renewal at least sixty (60) days prior to expiration of the then-current term.

3.3 Termination for Convenience. Either party may terminate this Agreement for convenience upon
ninety (90) days' prior written notice to the other party.

3.4 Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if
the other party: (a) materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within thirty (30)
days after receiving written notice; or (b) becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy protection.

3.5 Effect of Termination. Upon termination, Client shall pay for all services performed and expenses
incurred prior to the effective date of termination.

4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

4.1 Client Materials. Client retains all intellectual property rights in materials provided to Service
Provider ("Client Materials"). Client grants Service Provider a limited license to use Client Materials
solely to perform the services.

4.2 Work Product. Subject to full payment, Service Provider assigns to Client all right, title, and
interest in any custom deliverables created specifically for Client under a SOW ("Work Product").

4.3 Service Provider IP. Service Provider retains ownership of its pre-existing intellectual property,
tools, methodologies, and general know-how ("Service Provider IP"). Service Provider grants Client a
non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use Service Provider IP embedded in Work Product.

4.4 Restrictions. Client shall not reverse engineer, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive source
code from any Service Provider IP.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY

5.1 Definition. "Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by one party
("Disclosing Party") to the other party ("Receiving Party") that is designated as confidential or that
reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information.

5.2 Obligations. Each Receiving Party shall: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence using
at least the same degree of care used for its own confidential information (but no less than reasonable
care); (b) not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent; and
(c) use Confidential Information solely for purposes of this Agreement.

5.3 Term. Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.

6. WARRANTIES AND REPRESENTATIONS

6.1 Mutual Warranties. Each party represents and warrants that: (a) it has full power and authority to
enter into this Agreement; and (b) this Agreement does not conflict with any other agreement to which
it is a party.

6.2 Service Provider Warranty. Service Provider warrants that services will be performed in a
professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

6.3 DISCLAIMER. EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, NEITHER PARTY MAKES ANY WARRANTIES,
EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION ANY IMPLIED WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR
FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

7. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

7.1 EXCLUSION OF CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES. IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE FOR ANY
INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, OR CONSEQUENTIAL DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS, EVEN IF ADVISED
OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

7.2 LIABILITY CAP. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATED TO THIS
AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE GREATER OF: (A) THE TOTAL FEES PAID BY CLIENT IN THE TWELVE (12)
MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM; OR (B) $50,000.

8. GENERAL PROVISIONS

8.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of
the State of Washington, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

8.2 Dispute Resolution. Any disputes shall first be submitted to good-faith mediation before either
party may initiate litigation or arbitration proceedings.

8.3 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered by email with confirmation, overnight courier,
or certified mail to the addresses set forth on the signature page.

8.4 Entire Agreement. This Agreement, together with all SOWs, constitutes the entire agreement between
the parties with respect to its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations and agreements.

8.5 Amendment. This Agreement may only be amended by a written instrument signed by authorized
representatives of both parties.

8.6 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is found invalid or unenforceable, the remaining
provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

8.7 Waiver. No waiver of any breach shall be deemed a waiver of any subsequent breach.
